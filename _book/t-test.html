Part 3 T-test

This chapter shows how to the three T-Tests for Independent Samples (3.1), Paired Samples (3.2) and One Sample (3.3).

3.1 Independent Samples T-Test

  1. To perform an Independent Samples T-Test you need an outcome variable (continous) and a grouping variable with two levels. Therefore, your data should look something like this:

  2. Select Analyses -> T-Tests -> Independent Samples T-Test.

  3. Move your outcome variable to Dependent Variables and your grouping variable to Grouping Variable.

  4. The result is shown in the right panel

3.2 Paired Samples T-Test

  1. To perform a Paired Samples T-Test you need two continous variables. Therefore, your data should look something like this:

  2. Select Analyses -> T-Tests -> Paired Samples T-Test.

  3. Move your outcome variables to Dependent variables.

  4. The result is shown in the right panel

3.3 One Sample T-Test

  1. To perform a One Sample T-Test you need a continous variable and a mean to test against. Therefore, your data should look something like this:

  2. Select Analyses -> T-Tests -> One Sample T-Test.

  3. Move your dependent variable to Dependent Variables.

  4. Locate the section “Hypothesis” further down, this is where you enter the mean you want to test against.

  5. The result is shown in the right panel
